Why the Insurer's First Offer Is Almost Always Too Low

After an accident an adjuster often calls quickly, sounds sympathetic, and makes an early offer. Remember whose side they are on: the adjuster's job is to close your claim for as little as possible. Here is how to protect what your case is truly worth.

The early offer is a strategy

A fast offer lands before you know the full extent of your injuries, your future treatment costs, or your lost income. Once you sign a release you almost never get a second chance, even if your condition worsens.

Tactics to watch for

  • Recorded statements used to lock in, and later twist, your words
  • Requests for sweeping access to your entire medical history
  • Pressure to settle "today" for a one-time amount
  • Labelling your injury "minor" to apply Alberta's compensation cap

What actually maximizes your recovery

  • Complete medical documentation of every injury and its impact
  • A full accounting of lost income, future care, and out-of-pocket costs
  • Proper valuation of pain and suffering, not the insurer's number
  • A lawyer ready to go to court if the offer is unfair
