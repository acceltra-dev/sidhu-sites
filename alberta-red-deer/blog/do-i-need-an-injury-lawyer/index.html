Do You Need a Lawyer? How Representation Changes Your Payout

You can handle an injury claim yourself, but the real question is whether you'll recover what you're truly owed. Insurers settle differently with someone who has a lawyer prepared to go to court, and that difference goes straight into your pocket.

What a lawyer actually does for your claim

  • Values your claim properly, including future losses you may not have considered
  • Gathers the medical and financial evidence that supports a higher award
  • Handles every adjuster call, deadline, and document
  • Pushes back on the "minor injury" label and lowball offers
  • Takes the case to court when the insurer won't be fair

"But can I afford it?"

Injury claims are handled on a contingency basis: no upfront fees, and we only get paid if we win. There is no financial risk to having a professional fight for your maximum recovery.

When to call

The earlier the better, evidence is fresher and deadlines are safe. If you were injured in Red Deer or anywhere in Alberta, a free case review costs you nothing and could be worth a great deal.
