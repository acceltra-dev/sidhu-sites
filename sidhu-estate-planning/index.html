Our services

Complete estate planning in BC

Last will and testament

A legally binding document directing how your estate is distributed after death. The cornerstone of any estate plan.

Power of attorney

Appoint someone to manage your financial affairs if you become incapacitated. Gives your family the authority to act on your behalf.

Representation agreement

Appoint someone to make healthcare and personal decisions on your behalf if you are unable to. BC's equivalent of a healthcare directive.

Estate administration

We guide executors through the probate process step by step, from filing to final distribution, so nothing is missed.

Trust planning

Protect assets for minor children or family members with special needs. A trust ensures your wishes are carried out over time.

Wills variation

Challenge or defend a will that fails to adequately provide for a spouse or child. We represent both applicants and estates.

Common questions

What BC families ask us most

How much does a will cost in BC?

Fixed fees vary by complexity. A simple will starts at a few hundred dollars. We quote upfront before any work begins.

Do I need a lawyer to make a will in BC?

No, but a lawyer-drafted will is far less likely to be challenged or rejected by the courts. DIY wills frequently contain errors that cost families far more to fix.

What happens if I die without a will in BC?

Your estate is distributed according to BC's intestacy rules, which may not reflect your wishes. The government decides who gets what.

What is a representation agreement?

It appoints someone to make healthcare and personal care decisions for you if you cannot make them yourself. It is BC's equivalent of a healthcare directive.

Can I update my will after it is made?

Yes. We recommend reviewing your will every 3 to 5 years or after major life events like marriage, divorce, having children, or acquiring property.

How long does estate planning take?

Most clients complete a full estate plan in 2 to 3 appointments over 1 to 2 weeks.
