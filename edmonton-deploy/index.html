Know What You Are Up Against

What the Insurance Company Is Doing Right Now

From the moment your accident is reported, the insurer's team goes to work. Here is exactly what they are doing, and how we counter it on your behalf.

Tactic 01

They call you immediately for a recorded statement

Adjusters reach out within hours of an accident, while you are still in shock or on pain medication. Anything you say can be used to minimize your claim.

We tell you exactly what to say and what not to say before you speak to anyone from the insurance company.

Tactic 02

They make a quick settlement offer before you know your injuries

Soft tissue damage, chronic pain, and psychological impacts can take weeks or months to fully appear. Accepting early permanently closes your claim, even if your condition worsens.

We never let you settle until the full extent of your injuries and losses is documented and understood.

Tactic 03

They hire their own medical examiner

Insurers use so-called independent medical examiners who are paid by the insurer and routinely produce reports that downplay injuries. They are not on your side.

We challenge biased medical reports with independent experts and build a medical case that reflects your real condition.

Tactic 04

They monitor your social media

A single photo, check-in, or post can be taken out of context and used to argue you are not as injured as you claim. This is standard practice.

We advise you on digital privacy from day one so nothing can be used against you.

Tactic 05

They delay and exhaust you into settling for less

Insurance companies know that financial pressure and uncertainty wear people down. A lowball offer after months of delay looks more attractive when bills are piling up.

We move aggressively, keep your case on track, and take the financial pressure off by working on full contingency.

Tactic 06

They count on you never calling a lawyer

Insurers know that unrepresented claimants almost always settle for far less. Handling it alone is exactly what they want, because no one is holding them accountable for what your claim is truly worth.

The moment Alberta Accident Law is on your side, the balance shifts. Get your free, no-obligation case review and find out what your claim is really worth before you accept a single dollar.

Local Knowledge

Injury Claims in Edmonton: What to Know

Edmonton's high-speed ring road, river-valley arterials, and hard winter driving put thousands of people in collisions every year. If you have been hurt in the capital region, here is the local context that shapes your claim.

Where Edmonton crashes happen

Anthony Henday Drive, Whitemud Drive, and the Yellowhead Trail see the region's most serious high-speed collisions, while Gateway Boulevard, Calgary Trail, and the 23rd Avenue interchanges produce heavy city-traffic crashes. Winter ice on the Henday and the Quesnell Bridge is a frequent factor.

Where your claim is heard

Edmonton injury lawsuits are filed at the Edmonton Law Courts on Sir Winston Churchill Square, in the Court of King's Bench of Alberta. Under Alberta's Limitations Act you generally have 2 years from the date of your accident to start a claim, so acting early matters.

Getting treated

Serious injuries in Edmonton are most often treated at the University of Alberta Hospital and the Royal Alexandra Hospital, with the Grey Nuns and Misericordia community hospitals also handling trauma. Keep every medical record and receipt, they are central to proving your claim.
