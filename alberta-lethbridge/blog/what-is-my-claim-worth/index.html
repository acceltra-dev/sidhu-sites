What Is Your Injury Claim Really Worth in Alberta?

Most people badly underestimate what their injury claim is worth, which is exactly what the insurance company is counting on. In Alberta, full compensation spans several categories, and a strong claim accounts for every one of them.

What you can recover

  • Pain and suffering (general damages) for the physical and emotional toll
  • Past and future loss of income, including reduced earning capacity
  • Cost of future care: treatment, medication, and assistive devices
  • Out-of-pocket expenses, from physiotherapy to mileage to home help
  • Loss of housekeeping capacity when you can no longer do what you used to

The "minor injury" cap, and why the label matters

Alberta places an annually-adjusted cap on pain-and-suffering awards for injuries classed as "minor" (certain sprains, strains, and whiplash). Insurers push that label because it limits what they pay. Many injuries do not meet the definition, and proving it is often where the real money is won or lost.
